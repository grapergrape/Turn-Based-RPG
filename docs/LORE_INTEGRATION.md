# Lore Integration

This file explains how to keep the setting consistent inside gameplay, content, enemy design, dialogue, and quests.

The full canon lives in:

- `docs/lore/the_host_story_bible.md`

## Core canon summary

Humanity's future civilization became technologically advanced and hyper-religious. A Europa drilling mission exposed scientists and Father Marius Vale to an alien organism. Because Vale was the first meaningful human imprint, every Earthborn descendant of the organism carries **the Vale Imprint**.

The organism is called **The Host**.

The Host does not create random mutations. It turns infected humans into hellish religious body horror forms filtered through Father Vale's inherited vision of damnation.

## Design rule for Host enemies

Every Host enemy should answer this question:

> What kind of damnation-role has the Vale Imprint forced this person into?

Examples:

- A soldier becomes a Penitent Bastion, armored and unable to flee.
- A caregiver becomes a Madonna of Ash, preserving corpses as false children.
- A scientist becomes a Scholastic Heretic, dissecting everything in search of proof.
- A judge becomes a tribunal creature with many mouths.
- A pilot becomes a Seraphim Wreck, a broken angel of pressure suit and nerve-light.

Do not design Host enemies as generic zombies, bugs, slime aliens, or random tentacle monsters. They can have alien biology, but the silhouette and horror language should be infernal, saint-like, martyr-like, or ecclesiastical.

## Visual language for The Host

Use combinations of:

- Bone halos.
- Thorn growths.
- Fused prayer hands.
- Wet scripture-like nerves.
- Black-gold Europa tissue.
- Extra mouths that chant or confess.
- Faces stretched into agony masks.
- Wings made from ribs, skin, torn muscle, or ship cabling.
- Candle/fat/bone growths.
- Bell-shaped organs.
- Flesh arranged like robes, vestments, or burial cloth.
- Ship debris embedded like relics.
- Stained-glass eye clusters.
- Kneeling, cruciform, or processional body shapes.

The rule:

> Nothing should look like a normal alien monster. Everything should look like something trying to be holy and failing horribly.

## Factions should be morally complicated

Avoid making factions cartoonishly good or evil.

### Holy Remnant

Believes The Host is demonic. Preserves safety, literacy, medicine, and order, but uses censorship, purges, and religious authoritarianism.

### Lumen Compact

Believes The Host is biological. Preserves science and seeks cures, but performs unethical experiments and may sacrifice communities for data.

### Free Cities

Practical city-states focused on survival, trade, water, walls, and politics. They can be democratic, corrupt, generous, cruel, or all of those at once.

### Choir of the Open Wound

Worships The Host as revelation. Shelters outcasts and Bloomborn, but also performs infection rituals and wants to continue the Bloom.

### Ash Cartels

Bandits, smugglers, salvage gangs, and caravan powers. Some are monsters; some keep trade routes alive.

## Dialogue tone

Dialogue should sound grounded, desperate, political, and specific.

Good:

> "Keep your gloves on inside the chapel. The last man who touched the bell rope came back with six hands and no face."

Weak:

> "Whoa, that's one crazy alien zombie!"

Good:

> "The Pontifex calls it Hell because Hell gives him jurisdiction. The Compact calls it biology because biology gives them ownership. Either way, people vanish into laboratories and confession pits."

Weak:

> "Religion bad, science good."

## Gameplay integration ideas

The lore should influence systems:

- Fear and panic can increase Host risk.
- Faith, discipline, sedatives, or companion support can stabilize characters.
- Host contamination should be a slow-risk state, not always instant death.
- Some factions punish contamination; others study or worship it.
- Enemy designs should reflect past identity and Vale-Imprint damnation roles.
- Quests should often force choices between truth, survival, faith, and exploitation.

## Originality guardrails

The story uses familiar CRPG ingredients: factions, forbidden archives, cults, political hubs, and a late truth about the world. Those pieces are allowed only when the cause-and-effect chain stays specific to this setting. Use `docs/story/originality.md` as the longer audit when designing major quests, bosses, and faction branches.

The core chain is:

1. First contact was filtered through Father Marius Vale.
2. The Host inherited Vale's fear of Hell as biological grammar.
3. Sorell saved humanity by turning that fear into the Stilling lock.
4. The Choir mistakes Host-shaped doctrine for divine revelation.
5. The Devil manifestation is not a generic demon boss. It is the Host making Remnant fear into flesh.
6. The endgame is not "kill the source." It is deciding whether Vale's torture remains the price of human survival.

Avoid these drift risks:

- A lore hunt where the Three Witnesses are just collectible exposition.
- A cult that is evil because cultists are evil.
- A Remnant that is only a bad church.
- A Compact that is only good science or only mad science.
- A final boss that is only a large monster.
- A supernatural answer that cancels the biological and psychological horror.
- A faction ending that ignores Vale and Sorell's chamber.

Every major quest should make at least one ordinary human need matter: food, medicine, citizenship, road access, safety, shelter, grief, fear, or law. The horror works because it grows through those needs.

## Names

Use serious, grounded names.

Good names:

- The Host
- Vale Imprint
- Penitent Bastion
- Madonna of Ash
- Scholastic Heretic
- Seraphim Wreck
- Veyr's Gate
- Meridian Vault
- Sanctum Aurelian
- The Woundfall Zone

Avoid goofy names or placeholder names in committed content.

## Retcon rule

Do not casually rewrite core canon. If a change affects The Host, Father Vale, the Vale Imprint, the Europa mission, or the cause of the apocalypse, update the story bible deliberately and mention the change in a commit or PR summary.
