## Censure attendant shrine

At `(136, 48)`, a pre-Bloom field-attendant cradle stands on cemetery earth
between the two graveyard chapels. Remnant technicians added the credential
plate now fixed to its old service housing. The physical cradle predates the
Ashen Censure. Its use marker is at `(136, 49)`, and the issued frame unfolds
onto `(137, 48)`.

The shrine recognizes the player as an agent of Censure, asks where their
attendant is, and accepts the answer that none was issued. Its catalogue lists
Dominion Siege Drone Mark X, Palatine Combat Drone Mark VI, and Utility Drone
Mark I. The first selection fails because there is no siege cradle. The second
fails because its remote cradle is empty. Each failed line remains crossed out
for the run. Only the local Utility Drone Mark I can unfold, after which the
player must name it. No other model is physically shown at this site.

Registration is universal rather than an Engineer-only reward. The shrine
issues two Drone Service Parts. Eight more are distributed through the rejected
issue crate, condemned annex parts, written-off freight, Compact field satchel,
and hidden-rows clinic satchel. Engineering opens the deepest general paths,
while Medicine can carry the Medical branch and Stealth can carry the Veil
branch.

## Deborah Carbo's account

Deborah Carbo waits at `(118,47)` beside spilled charcoal and the detached wheel her
robbers left across her foot. She is not infected. The cult band tests her for
Remnant loyalty, finds none, and decides that killing her has no ritual or
political value. They take the cart because it can be sold.

The cart trail passes the cultist and Host-wolf kill site before reaching the
Stage IV lure at `(76,35)`. White hub cord and spilled charcoal let a player who
spoke with Deborah identify the trap without a field-rating check. Springing the
ambush reveals a flat purse at `(78,36)` containing twenty ducats.

Deborah pockets all twenty on return, withholds her promised payment, and claims
that five more are missing. The player can pay the revised amount, refuse it, or
end the account with a threat. Each route records a separate outcome flag and
completes the optional `edda-farrs-account` quest.
