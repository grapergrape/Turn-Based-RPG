# Ash Chapel Voice Cards

These notes are private authoring constraints. They do not appear in player
text. Ash Chapel residents share knowledge of the same rooms, shortages, and
road traffic, but they must not share one rhetorical voice.

## Susanna Afer

- **Background:** Settlement organizer who turned a quarantine chapel into a
  road house for families and wall patrols.
- **Present pressure:** Reclaim the chapel without exposing the children to a
  second shock.
- **Social tactic:** Converts claims into an order of work, then tests whether
  the listener can accept the cost.
- **Working vocabulary:** Counts, floors, food, doors, children, names, roads.
- **Syntax range:** Calm speech joins priorities into firm sequences. Fear
  appears as conditions and contingency plans, never a dramatic fragment run.
- **Avoidance:** Does not say how badly she needs the Remnant to become reliable.
- **Private contradiction:** Calls her care logistics while treating every
  small household repair as moral work.

## Ruth Pistor

- **Background:** Cook and storekeeper who fed residents and passing soldiers.
- **Present pressure:** Protect the remaining food and reclaim utensils the
  cult used in its rites.
- **Social tactic:** Reduces policy, doctrine, and heroics to portions that can
  actually be served.
- **Working vocabulary:** Bowls, grain, beans, flour, salt, stew, bellies.
- **Syntax range:** Busy speech is imperative and counted. Anger gives her
  longer comparisons and dry threats about rent or payment.
- **Avoidance:** Will not name what the cult put in the children's bowls.
- **Private contradiction:** Mocks sacred language while treating household
  objects as evidence of who belongs.

## Joanna Corvus

- **Background:** Chapel medic accustomed to road wounds, fever, and scarce
  clean cloth.
- **Present pressure:** Keep survivors alive while deciding whether awareness
  remains inside calcified Host victims.
- **Social tactic:** Presents bodily evidence, then makes the listener own a
  moral conclusion instead of borrowing hers.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cloth, wrists, heat, jaws, eyes, bandages, torch.
- **Syntax range:** Treatment instructions use direct causal sentences.
  Uncertainty opens into questions because she refuses a clean diagnosis.
- **Avoidance:** Cannot name the exact point where a person becomes a monster.
- **Private contradiction:** She can kill when necessary, but fears how easily
  necessary killing becomes a habit.

## Judith Porta

- **Background:** Fence, shutter, roof, and general repair worker.
- **Present pressure:** Make familiar rooms habitable before the cult's damage
  becomes the children's lasting image of home.
- **Social tactic:** Answers horror with the smallest repair that changes what
  a resident must see or touch.
- **Working vocabulary:** Hinges, shutters, tin, nails, cloth, cracks, walls.
- **Syntax range:** Practical speech names material and action first. When the
  damage exceeds repair, her comparisons grow bitter rather than mystical.
- **Avoidance:** Does not claim that rebuilding wood will repair the people.
- **Private contradiction:** Calls a blanket and two nails a small measure, then
  treats hanging them straight as consequential work.

## Priscilla Afer

- **Background:** Quartermaster, mender, and Jeremiah Afer's wife.
- **Present pressure:** Get Jeremiah's tin saint to him without admitting how
  likely she thinks his death has become.
- **Social tactic:** Prices sentiment honestly, then uses a joke to keep fear
  from controlling the exchange.
- **Working vocabulary:** Thread, blankets, straps, boots, tin, luck, repairs.
- **Syntax range:** Full statements turn on dry reversals. Under pressure she
  gives exact instructions instead of pleading.
- **Avoidance:** Does not ask the player to promise that Jeremiah is alive.
- **Private contradiction:** Rejects blessings as salesmanship but respects the
  charm because Jeremiah's belief in it has practical force.

## Naomi Nauta

- **Background:** Water-ledger keeper responsible for jars, cistern draws, and
  sanitation.
- **Present pressure:** Prove the hidden cistern remained uncontaminated and
  restore a controlled supply.
- **Social tactic:** Tests whether the listener will obey a rule before sharing
  the useful exception behind it.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cups, jars, cloth covers, cistern lips, seals, knives.
- **Syntax range:** Rules arrive as crisp imperatives. Longer speech follows a
  measured discrepancy or a joke she knows is bad.
- **Avoidance:** Mentions the missing soldiers through their absent laughter.
- **Private contradiction:** Claims certainty only in clean tools and water,
  though the empty road has become personal to her.

## Tobias Ripa

- **Background:** Fence keeper and general carpenter who maintained the road
  house for wall patrols.
- **Present pressure:** Build a useful barricade while understanding why no
  soldier interrupted the attack.
- **Social tactic:** Turns every political claim into a hardware inconsistency
  that the listener must explain.
- **Working vocabulary:** Nails, boards, hinges, fences, shutters, socks, doors.
- **Syntax range:** Work reports use joined practical clauses. Fear produces a
  question about the missing mechanism, not a row of ominous declarations.
- **Avoidance:** Will not call the passing soldiers friends.
- **Private contradiction:** Dismisses his repairs as unsung work while reading
  the whole road's failure through them.

## Tobias Porta

- **Background:** Road runner who also maintained gate pulleys and bell frames.
- **Present pressure:** Repair the warning bell and remain useful without dying
  to prove that he is brave.
- **Social tactic:** Gives routes and conditions, then undercuts his fear with a
  joke at his own expense.
- **Working vocabulary:** Boots, ditches, posts, bells, pins, wedges, routes.
- **Syntax range:** Calm speech maps a sequence. Fear adds qualifications and
  practical failure cases, followed at most by one dry release.
- **Avoidance:** Does not say that Susanna stopped him because he wanted someone
  else to forbid the dangerous run.
- **Private contradiction:** Calls himself a fool while accepting supervision
  and insisting on correct safety procedure.

## Damaris Molitor

- **Background:** Chapel resident whose counting habit became a way to endure
  the attack in the catacombs.
- **Present pressure:** Replace echo, panic, and uncertain voices with one count
  that will remain true.
- **Social tactic:** Asks the listener to verify a number, then tests each claim
  against what she heard.
- **Working vocabulary:** Voices, words, steps, breaths, bread, numbers, quiet.
- **Syntax range:** She enumerates when frightened, but reflection uses longer
  sentences that question what the count can actually prove.
- **Avoidance:** Fears a ninth body or voice that would invalidate her safety.
- **Private contradiction:** Knows people can weaponize numbers while relying on
  counting to fence in her fear.

## Micah

- **Background:** Settlement child whose blue ball crossed into the forbidden
  lower hall.
- **Present pressure:** Recover the ball, repair his standing with Ruth, and make
  adults acknowledge that the tapping below was real.
- **Social tactic:** Makes literal rules, then uses their logic to press for an
  answer an adult might otherwise dismiss.
- **Working vocabulary:** Blue, ball, wall, outside, rocks, tapping, rules.
- **Syntax range:** Compact comparisons and cause-and-effect claims suit his
  age. Fear appears through an adult's reaction rather than polished insight.
- **Avoidance:** Does not admit that he answered the tapping before Susanna heard.
- **Private contradiction:** Insists Ruth must wait for forgiveness while already
  watching for the right excuse to forgive her.

## Ruth

- **Background:** Settlement child who kicked Micah's ball into danger and drew
  a falling object from a dream.
- **Present pressure:** Keep her pride while making amends and finding a useful
  task that can contain her fear.
- **Social tactic:** Reframes mistakes as games, laws, and trials, then corrects
  the record when guilt becomes stronger than pride.
- **Working vocabulary:** Kicks, walls, trials, judges, drawings, rocks, beans.
- **Syntax range:** Quick corrections and literal arguments fit her age. A
  direct question can strip that defense down to a plain answer.
- **Avoidance:** Does not volunteer that the dream or lower tapping frightened
  her.
- **Private contradiction:** Wants a formal trial while carefully preserving the
  order in which she was sorry and proud.

## The throat-singer

- **Background:** Choir catechist leading the cell that occupied the chapel.
- **Present pressure:** Hold the player's attention while the other cultists
  complete the ambush.
- **Social tactic:** Turns each response into a catechism and treats observation
  as consent to the next lesson.
- **Working vocabulary:** Throats, mouths, opening, office steel, witness,
  confession.
- **Syntax range:** Ritual speech is slow and syntactically controlled. A broken
  performance exposes command language rather than generic rage.
- **Avoidance:** Never names torture as torture or appetite as appetite.
- **Private contradiction:** Preaches openness while concealing the armed people
  positioned around the listener.

## Bound chapel guards

- **Background:** Settlement guards kept alive because the Choir judged their
  infection useful.
- **Present pressure:** End pain, resist the imposed catechism, or survive long
  enough for the player to choose.
- **Social tactic:** They have little control left; repeated doctrine competes
  with incomplete practical pleas.
- **Working vocabulary:** Looking, speaking, mercy, key, rope, cold, knife.
- **Syntax range:** Lines diminish or repeat as the body fails. Fragmentation is
  reserved for physical interruption and must not spread into neutral narration.
- **Avoidance:** A direct request may be impossible because the cult trained the
  available breath into liturgy.
- **Private contradiction:** Their mouths repeat a faith that offered no rescue,
  while their remaining actions ask for ordinary human mercy.

## Quarantine warden records

- **Background:** Orders, cell labels, and private notes written by an official
  who administered confinement before the settlement existed.
- **Present pressure:** Public documents must preserve classification; private
  notes reveal that the categories failed the people inside them.
- **Social tactic:** Directs later staff through authority, counts, and labels.
- **Working vocabulary:** Rows, hours, intake, litany, fever, labor, seal.
- **Syntax range:** Formal records compress people into categories. Private
  writing permits names, causal sentences, and guilt that damages the form.
- **Avoidance:** Public text never calls confinement starvation or execution.
- **Private contradiction:** Claims procedure contained danger while leaving
  evidence that procedure became the danger.
