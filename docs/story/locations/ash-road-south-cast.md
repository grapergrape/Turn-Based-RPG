# Ash Road South Cast and Street Dialogue

## Runtime scope

South Measure uses 109 individually planned identities. The 130 by 80 surface
holds 81 living NPCs. Its nine helper maps add 27 living identities and reuse
surface residents where their work or household continues indoors. Junia Lector,
the fixed Intake Clerk, is the final identity.

The helper maps contain 71 logical character placements: 70 walking actors and
Junia's wicket-bound presence. Forty-one placements support direct conversation.
Every logical helper placement has three authored barks, for 213 interior
lines. Across the whole location, 48 unique identities are interactable

## Street tone

Walk-by speech follows a 70 percent ordinary-work and domestic register, a 20
percent social-pressure register, and a 10 percent Host-dread register. Most
people talk about cups, canvas, bread, forms, relatives, tools, and the next
shift. Political language enters when a concrete need is being counted or
denied. Host dread appears as a noticed sound, bad water, road evidence, or a
memory somebody has work to do around.

Every NPC has three authored barks in the level data:

1. A routine line that identifies present work.
2. A pressure line that locates the person inside the camp’s conflict.
3. A personal or local line that connects them to a household, memory, or
   neighboring worker.

The runtime’s ambient cooldown permits only one nearby bark at a time. The
population should therefore read as overheard life, not eighty people speaking
at the player.

### Junia Lector and the Intake Clerk

Junia is counted as a character identity and conversation, but not as a walking
actor. Her human-sized body remains fixed through the existing
`intake-clerk-wicket` prop. Three rib mouths repeat admission codes and deferred
mercy phrases. Reading the original roll recovers the human name Junia Lector
without turning her into an oracle or lore dispenser.

Containment, Cassian's seal, Compact removal, controlled killing, burning, and
resealing are separate authored outcomes. Every terminal route sets
`intake-clerk-resolved`. The isolated resident loop requires the annex bypass
route and an outcome that leaves the manifold usable. Forcing the wicket
releases Junia as a human-sized conditional combat actor. Using Cassian's full seal
keeps him on South Measure watch for the next departure cycle.

### Salome Naso, undercover Choir agent

Salome's surface presentation remains unchanged: an ambient after-dark teacher
who speaks about letters, cards, and Rhoda's favorite shape. She becomes
interactable only in the charity cellar. Her cover is useful, patient work with
adults rejected by the clinic or trapped by debt. She does not recruit children
in this plot.

Salome is early Stage III and keeps the change quiet with diverted suppressant.
If exposed, the dedicated False Catechist sprite preserves her human scale and
teacher clothing. A narrow sternum mouth, small teeth, one lifted rib edge, a
beginning prayer grip, and a second confessional voice sit beneath the normal
silhouette. The reveal is a short optional encounter, not a second location
boss.

The confrontation requires any two of four clues:

- `neri-copy-hand-found`
- `neri-suppressant-diversion-found`
- `neri-recruit-list-found`
- `neri-host-sign-seen`

Evidence comes from the burned labels, medicine cabinet, recruit lesson sheet,
Matthias, Jair, Thecla, Salome, or direct medical observation. Medicine is a shortcut
to one clue and is never required for progress. `lesson-under-the-wrap` stays
hidden until any clue sets `neri-investigation-started`.

The exclusive outcomes are:

- `neri-agent-barred`
- `neri-agent-council`

## Conversation coverage

The six principal conversations contain eight to eleven nodes each. They cover
the main water-roll scene, throttled-flow diagnosis, Compact terms, Morrow terms,
charity-cot compromise, Cassian’s Ash Chapel withdrawal, Brother Job, and the
Hallowfen reply code. Return choices recognize evidence gathered from the
arrival fires, charity cots, freight crews, return shelf, Faber family, hidden
households, Compact applicants, annex workers, grave keeper, and north papers.

The 25 secondary conversations stay compact. Twenty-four contain three nodes
covering the NPC’s work and local pressure. Jeremiah Afer’s five-node scene closes
Priscilla’s necklace errand without changing a helper-map or faction outcome.

The helper maps add 41 character conversations. Most are short work,
household, care, or record scenes. Susanna's hall conversation carries the water
plan, authority, custody, and assembly sequence. Junia and Salome have the larger
stateful graphs required by their optional investigations. Three additional
object conversations record Salome evidence and do not count as character
conversations.
