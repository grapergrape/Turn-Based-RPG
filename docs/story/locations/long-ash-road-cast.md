# Long Ash Road Voice Cards

These private cards govern the named speakers on the road approach. The Carbo
brothers share a century of family humor, but their jokes must reveal different
defenses. Deborah's account language must never sound like either brother.

## Deborah Carbo

- **Background:** Independent charcoal carter who moves small loads between
  cook fires, smiths, and road settlements.
- **Present pressure:** Recover her cart money, conceal fear behind an account,
  and extract whatever extra payment the situation permits.
- **Social tactic:** Makes the listener enter her ledger, withholds one relevant
  fact at a time, then changes the count when the balance favors her.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cart, wheel, axle, cord, wax, purse, count, account.
- **Syntax range:** Uses compact transactional clauses joined by comparison or
  correction. Pain shortens her patience, not her grammar.
- **Avoidance:** Will not call survival a blessing or offer sincere gratitude.
- **Private contradiction:** Claims belief is worthless while using the shape of
  prayer, fairness, and confession whenever they improve a bargain.

## Gideon Carbo

- **Background:** Calcified during the Stilling with both arms fixed in opposite
  directions. Later Holts planted him at the road fork because his pose could
  mark both routes, then used him as a speaking signpost.
- **Present pressure:** Get someone to check Eleazar and the family without letting
  concern become another public mark cut into him.
- **Social tactic:** Provokes the listener, judges the response, then hides a
  request inside commentary about roads and bad pilgrims.
- **Working vocabulary:** Directions, signs, coins, pilgrims, rain, mud, roads.
- **Syntax range:** Dry comparisons and qualified observations carry his humor.
  He uses a true fragment only when pain or news interrupts the performance.
- **Avoidance:** Does not describe how deeply the carving, spit, and jokes still
  injure him.
- **Private contradiction:** Rejects every promise of release while keeping a
  small verbal opening for surprise.

## Eleazar Carbo

- **Background:** Calcified during the Stilling and lashed above Carbo wheat as a
  living scarecrow by generations who later came to call him uncle.
- **Present pressure:** Learn whether the household survived and decide whether
  the Choir's praise made him complicit in its visit.
- **Social tactic:** Enlarges humiliation into a mock kingdom until the listener
  is willing to answer the family question directly.
- **Working vocabulary:** Wheat, crows, rope, cups, boots, harvests, kingship.
- **Syntax range:** His ordinary speech wanders through comic conditions and
  invented laws. Grief may stop him once, after which memory and practical
  instruction return.
- **Avoidance:** Does not directly ask whether his vanity helped the Choir harm
  the family.
- **Private contradiction:** Claims to believe in ascension, yet every imagined
  reward is an ordinary body at home without ropes.

## Keziah Fullo's records

- **Background:** Field and mortuary records made by a road official who learned
  that calcified people were answering familiar sounds deliberately.
- **Present pressure:** Preserve evidence inside an office that classifies every
  response as settling and prepares purgation.
- **Social tactic:** Uses measurements precise enough that a later reader can
  recover the conclusion she was not permitted to state.
- **Working vocabulary:** Response width, bell interval, pressure pin, subject
  number, familiar voice, name.
- **Syntax range:** Test records stay procedural. Marginal notes restore names
  and causal connections without becoming lyrical confession.
- **Avoidance:** Does not write a direct accusation the road office could remove
  with her access.
- **Private contradiction:** Continues using official forms while arranging the
  evidence to defeat the office's official judgment.
