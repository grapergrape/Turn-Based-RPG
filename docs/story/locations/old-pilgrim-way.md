# Old Pilgrim Way

## Status and campaign role

Old Pilgrim Way is the implemented Act I route after Ash Road South. It is a
120 by 70 road surface with five connected church and novitiate interiors. The
main route continues toward Quarantine Farms. The Hill Church branch is an
optional investigation and dungeon.

The location has three jobs:

- Carry South Measure's immediate political and character outcomes onto the
  next road.
- Put a recent Stage IV threat between ordinary travelers and the next food
  district.
- Preserve a pre-Bloom tragedy in which a working safety system became a tomb.

## Clergy and institutional history

The Hill Church was a Solar Ecclesiate road church before the Bloom. Priests
kept its altar and records. Nuns ran its hospice and taught the novices.
Pilgrims used the public nave, wash rooms, and buried refuge spaces. Priests and
nuns held the closure duties. The institution had no separate guard caste or
security order.

Sister Thecla and Father Noah are present-day Holy Remnant clergy using the road
and shelter. They did not serve the old institution. Their titles show the
continuity of religious work without implying that the current Remnant built
or operated the pre-Bloom complex.

## Surface crisis

Sister Thecla has closed northbound travel after Tobias Faber returned alone from
the east fields. Five dormant Stage IV forms lie in separate furrows among the
missing pilgrims. They retain the work and posture of a procession runner, bell
carrier, and cord bearer. Tobias's wounds, Host Signs, or Search can expose the
lead body before all five rise. Medicine can stabilize Tobias by spending a field
dressing. He then remembers the cart's release pin, which opens a flanking
route. Clearing the encounter and reporting the exact count opens the road
toward Quarantine Farms.

The surface carries one visible South Measure governance outcome:

- Timothy Cato for Compact administration.
- Gaius Cotta for Morrow control.
- Tobit Faber for resident control.
- Hannah of Bethel after the settlement is sealed.

Brother Cassian appears at the camp or church branch when his South Measure route
places him there. Noa Faber appears when she travels north with the school cards.
Choir influence and the one-season school outcome leave smaller environmental
evidence even when their related actors do not travel.

## The buried novitiate

Regional contamination alarms closed the buried pressure doors automatically.
The mechanism kept Bloom air and Host growth outside. It also kept priests,
sisters, novices, and sheltered pilgrims inside while it waited for an external
clearance that never arrived.

The emergency water ended on the fifth day. The last tally begins with 61 names
and records seven people alive on day nine. Empty tanks, a stripped pantry,
scraped vessels, a dead pump, a failed breach, and ordinary skeletons carry the
account. No victim transformed, and no living faction occupies the complex.
The closure duty roll, novitiate sleeping roll, trial candidate roll, and final
chapter record allow the player to reconstruct who was trapped below.

The dungeon route is:

1. Hill Church: prove the apse sits over a closure route, then release it.
2. Closure Stair: find the service key or bypass the inner pressure door.
3. Novitiate Quarters: compare the pump, cistern, ration marks, and last tally,
   then open the trial passage.
4. Trial Galleries: complete Quiet, Service, Burden, and Mercy through skill or
   a physical fallback. Earlier evidence can improve the intended solutions.
5. Sealed Chapter: read the final closure record, open the separate Oath
   Armory, take the Processional Pike, and use the return lift.

The Oath Armory contains no body. Its independent seal explains why the pike
survived while the congregation died outside it.

The final profession mechanism distinguishes an intact passage through all four
trials from a route forced through one or more machines. Back in the church,
the player decides what happens to the recovered names: public memorial tablets,
a copy for the northbound road book, or sealed return to the chapter. The choice
leaves a different physical arrangement in the church.

## Voice cards

### Sister Thecla

- **Background:** Present-day Remnant road sister managing a temporary shelter
  and the north chain.
- **Present pressure:** Count the missing field party before another household
  enters the furrows.
- **Social tactic:** Makes uncertainty physical with cups, then refuses to move
  the chain for anything less than an exact count.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cups, count, family, chain, road, cart, furrows.
- **Syntax range:** Instructions are firm and complete. Relief appears through a
  changed object or action rather than a speech about hope.
- **Avoidance:** Does not name the two people she expects the field to return.
- **Private contradiction:** Serves the Remnant while treating immediate family
  safety as more authoritative than any distant office.

### Father Noah

- **Background:** Present-day Remnant priest and record keeper sheltering in a
  church built by an older Ecclesiate institution.
- **Present pressure:** Recover the buried route and decide who may claim the
  names found below it.
- **Social tactic:** Places one material contradiction in front of the listener,
  then waits for the record to defeat the building's public story.
- **Working vocabulary:** Inventory, page, apse, stair, door, count, names, copy.
- **Syntax range:** Calm statements join evidence with its institutional
  consequence. He reserves a brief correction for the moment a category fails.
- **Avoidance:** Does not pretend that his current Church automatically owns the
  dead of the older one.
- **Private contradiction:** Works as a priest while repeatedly limiting the
  first word any religious office may have over the names.

### Tobias Faber

- **Background:** Carter who built his own release bed and returned from the
  east furrows with an injured arm.
- **Present pressure:** Keep the arm, account for Leah and Abner, and make his
  remembered cart mechanism useful to the rescue.
- **Social tactic:** Gives one route or hardware fact at a time and watches the
  listener's face for the verdict he is afraid to request.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cart, rut, pin, rail, bed, spokes, mule, binding.
- **Syntax range:** Shock makes his first report concrete but incomplete. After
  treatment, mechanical instructions recover their ordered causal form.
- **Avoidance:** Asks whether he keeps the arm before asking whether his
  companions live.
- **Private contradiction:** Trusts the cart he built more than his own memory,
  though memory is the only way anyone else can use it.

### Noa Faber on the north road

- **Background:** South Measure condenser mechanic carrying school cards toward
  the Farms after the settlement decision.
- **Present pressure:** Get real pupils through a document chain that may contain
  a false family line.
- **Social tactic:** Separates the valid person or need from the compromised
  paper around it.
- **Working vocabulary:** Cards, pupils, review, approval, table, line, names.
- **Syntax range:** Her road speech is spare because the decision has already
  been made. Each statement distinguishes two categories instead of performing
  generic stoicism.
- **Avoidance:** Does not reopen the family argument she left at South Measure.
- **Private contradiction:** Distrusts false paperwork but accepts a false family
  line when it keeps real pupils moving.

### Brother Cassian on the north road

- **Background:** Penitent Engine carrying Ash Chapel's audit and South
  Measure's signal history through damaged vox hardware.
- **Present pressure:** Determine whether the buried road count is another reply
  loop before it learns the settlement's rhythm.
- **Social tactic:** Touches the system, reports the signal, and withholds
  judgment until the next action is clear.
- **Working vocabulary:** Count, knock, seal, plate, signal, attendance, rhythm.
- **Syntax range:** Vox status phrases may remain clipped where the machine or
  protocol produces them. His analysis joins cause and duty in full clauses.
- **Avoidance:** Does not describe fear except as a repeatable signal risk.
- **Private contradiction:** Treats himself as an audit instrument while
  choosing routes according to which children might be woken or endangered.

Timothy Cato, Gaius Cotta, Hannah of Bethel, and Tobit Faber retain the cards in
`docs/story/locations/ash-road-south-cast.md`. Their Old Pilgrim lines change
present pressure, not personality: Timothy makes paperwork physically survivable,
Gaius marks ownership before moving freight, Hannah keeps one bedroll for the
unanswered idea of home, and Tobit refuses office ownership of resident labor.

## Canon boundaries

- Player-facing scenes do not reveal Europa, Father Vale, Seneca, or the Vale
  Imprint.
- The underground dead are ordinary and long dead. They never become a combat
  encounter.
- The trials are practical Ecclesiate mechanisms, not supernatural judgment.
- The Processional Pike is old equipment, not a sacred or contaminated magic
  weapon.
- Reaching the north boundary briefs Quarantine Farms but does not load a level
  that has not yet been implemented.

supporting actors, enemy, items, quests, and dialogues. Spatial references and
connector notes live in `docs/maps/act-1/old-pilgrim-way/`.
